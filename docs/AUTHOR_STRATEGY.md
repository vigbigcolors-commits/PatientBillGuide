# Author & EEAT Strategy

How to present authorship honestly for Google YMYL — without fake credentials.

---

## What Google actually wants (billing / finance YMYL)

Google does **not** require an MD or lawyer for medical **billing literacy** content.

Google **does** require:

1. **Honest identity** — who is behind the site
2. **Transparent expertise** — what you actually know (not invented)
3. **Editorial process** — how content is researched and updated
4. **Sourcing** — government data (CMS, HHS), not unsourced claims
5. **Limitations** — what the site cannot do

A solo founder with honest bio + strong methodology can rank. Fake "Medical Advisory Board" hurts more than it helps.

---

## What NOT to do

| ❌ Bad | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Claim MD / RN / JD without license | FTC + Google manual action risk |
| "Our team of billing experts" (fake) | EEAT violation if untrue |
| Stock photo doctor on About page | Deceptive trust signals |
| No author on CPT pages | Weak E for encyclopedia content |
| Full anonymous site | Weaker than named founder |

## Recommended approach for Vigen (solo founder)

### Use your real first name

**`Vigen`** is fine. Stronger than random pseudonym:

- Pseudonym alone → Google and users trust less
- Real first name + honest role → acceptable for consumer advocacy tools
- Optional: first name + last initial (`Vigen K.`) if privacy needed
- Optional: LinkedIn or GitHub link (huge EEAT boost)

### Official title (pick one — honest)

| Title | When to use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Founder & independent researcher** | ✅ Recommended default |
| Consumer health billing advocate | Good for empathetic tone |
| Software developer | Mention as *how* tools are built, not primary title |

**Do not use:** "Medical expert", "Billing specialist" (unless certified CPC certified — are you?)

### Sample author bio (`/authors/vigen/`)

> **Vigen** is the founder of BillTox and an independent researcher focused on U.S. medical billing transparency. He builds the tools on this site and writes plain-English guides to help patients understand hospital bills and insurance EOBs. BillTox is not a medical practice or law firm. All pricing data comes from public CMS releases; all content is reviewed against official government sources. [Editorial policy →]

Short. Honest. No fake degrees.

### "Reviewed by" without fake doctors

Instead of medical reviewer:

> **Fact-checked against:** CMS Physician Fee Schedule 2026, CMS NCCI Policy Manual Q1 2026

This is stronger than a fake MD for a **data-driven** site.

## Editorial policy connection

`/editorial-policy/` must state:

1. CPT summaries are **original plain-English**, not AMA copy
2. Prices sourced from **CMS MPFS** with version date
3. Billing flags use **CMS NCCI** rules
4. Content updated when CMS releases new data
5. Corrections welcome via `/contact/`
